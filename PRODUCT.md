# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Multiple users, each with their own private account and fully isolated data. A user wants full, private control over their personal finances without trusting a third-party app with bank credentials. Uses it on mobile daily and desktop occasionally. Already in a financial mindset when they open it — not browsing, not exploring. Here to log something, check a number, or review a period. Every interaction should feel fast and deliberate.

An **admin** (the first registered user) additionally manages accounts: creating users, resetting passwords, promoting/demoting admins, and removing accounts. Admins do not see other users' financial data — management is limited to the accounts themselves plus aggregate counts.

## Product Purpose

A private, self-hosted personal finance tracker for an Indian user banking with HDFC. Tracks expenses, income, investments, and subscriptions. Imports real transactions from Gmail automatically. Gives a clear picture of monthly cashflow, budget adherence, and investment commitments without requiring manual entry of every rupee.

Success looks like: open the app, understand your financial position in under 10 seconds, close it.

## Brand Personality

Safe. Minimalistic. Polished.

The app handles real money. It should feel like a precision instrument — not a consumer product trying to be friendly, not a bank trying to seem trustworthy through navy and gold. Quiet confidence. Nothing decorative that doesn't also inform.

## Anti-references

- AI slop: gradient hero cards with a big number and glowing accent rings, identical icon-grid cards, glassmorphism used decoratively, navy + gold because "fintech"
- Busy dashboards that show every metric at once
- Consumer-banking apps that over-explain and add friction to feel "helpful"
- Anything that looks like a Dribbble shot — visually impressive, functionally hollow

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers first.** Every screen's primary job is to surface one clear number or insight. Supporting context is secondary. Never bury the signal.
2. **Earn every pixel.** If a visual element doesn't carry information or aid navigation, remove it. Decoration is a tax on attention.
3. **Private by default.** The app handles sensitive data. The UI should feel contained and discreet — not broadcast-ready or demo-friendly.
4. **Precision over warmth.** This is a tool, not a companion. Copy is direct, interactions are snappy, feedback is immediate. No softening language.
5. **Invisible polish.** Details should compound into a feeling of quality, not announce themselves. Transitions should be noticed only if they're missing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected (already implemented). Sufficient contrast in both light and dark modes. Touch targets meet 44px minimum on mobile.
